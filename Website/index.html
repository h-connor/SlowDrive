What is it all about?

Each feature was carefully developed for your convenience :)

Get sound notifications when going too fast

You will receive sound notifications every time you exceed the speed limit for more than 10 kilometers per hour (or miles per hour). The sound, carefully picked to please you ears, will gently notify you to slow down.

Track your speed and speed limit

On the main screen of the app you can easily track your current speed of your favorite units as well as the speed limit on the street where you drive. It will also warns you if you have previously oversped in the region. Use the start/stop button to launch the system and quickly pause it case you don't need it at the moment.

Flexible settings to meet all your needs

Flexible settings page will allow you to change your email and password with a simplicity of one click. No email confirmations or re-captchas! Change your preferred metric units, turn the sound notifications on and off, change volume, play with settings as if it was Angry Birds. One of our favorite features is request in background. You can minimize the app and still get sound notifications if going too fast.

Secure authentication to save all your data

We care about your safety. That is why we introduced a hashing function that will create a set of unreadable characters from your password. Nothing else other than an app is able to recognized the modified password. So you can be sure that your speeding history will not get to the government:)

How does it work

Vision statement

SlowDrive - Our VisionSlow
Drive is an application system that will be used to notify drivers if they are speeding. Similar to a radar speed gun, this application measures the speed of the driver, and in addition, indicates as to whether or not the driver is over the speed limit.Our ValuesSlowDrive main goal is to improve road safety and securing driver's finances. Mainly by reducing the risk of road accidents cause by over or under speeding and preventing drivers from getting speeding ticket.
‍Audience
Anyone driving a vehicle will be capable of using this system. This includes but is not limited to, students learning to drive, people driving to or from work, cab drivers, bus drivers, people driving from a bar; whether this is in a motorized or automotive vehicle.
‍Our Plan
The system is primarily designed to track the speed of the driver while having knowledge of the speed limit in the area of the driver’s current location. It will allow the speed of the driver to be viewed with a distinguishable form of telling the driver if they need to slow down.The system will be used to reduce potential dangers or tickets that rise from speeding. It will tell the driver with sounds or vibrations indicating that the driver should slow down. Since the system only considers the speed of the driver, it does not need to recognize what type of vehicle the driver is in. It will need to maintain a method of retrieving speed limits in specific areas of the driver’s location.This system will be an improvement over current systems in use, which supports limited features to indicate changes in speed limits over unknown areas while being within different interfaces; most notable being the speedometer in front of the steering wheel of a car, with some newer car models supporting speed limit display on a screen viewable to the driver. As a result of our system, further functionality can be provided such as the user being directly informed of exceeding the speed limit. In addition, our system offers further flexibility by being independent of the vehicle in use. That is, a driver can use this system in any vehicle.
‍Medium
This system is visualized to be a mobile application on any android device. Future releases may include compatibility to come with a vehicle itself where the application is embedded in the vehicle’s system, or with any form of a mobile device that is commonly near a driver such as an iPhone.
‍Measuring Success
An indication of success for the application is the decrease of road accidents by 5% for the first year after initially publishing the application. This will be done by the ease of use for the application and elimination of distractions. In order to reduce the risk of distracted driving and encourage the ease of use, our system does not require hands-on use whilst in the process of driving. That is, our system does not require any use from the user in order to function other than opening our system.This will help meet the goal of reducing the risk of accidents as well as prevent drivers from getting a speeding ticket and thus, improving driver’s safety and securing finances.

The process of creation

Iteration 1

Iteration 1

We started with the small

The first thing we made to work was a simple speedometer. Using a YouTube guide to understand how to call the Android location. We made our version of a speedometer that showed speed in kilometers per hour instead of default meters per second.

How to find an API?

If you have an experience in application development, you probably know that it is challenging to find a suitable API for your needs. For our app, it had to be free and return a speed limit based on longitude and latitude. Simple requirements, but hard to find. We spend about two weeks looking for a good API.

A lot to figure out

We were also planning on the future project architecture. Which classes do we need and how they will communicate to each other.

Iteration 2

Iteration 2

Slowly but surely

After searching the entire Internet trying to find a free map API, we were choosing between Google and Bing. It appeared that Bing is more loyal to developers and provide a free API just as we need. So, we decided to go with it.

Further modifications

We improved the UI, added user authentication and the landing page. The app started to work as expected.

Still not perfect

During the Iteration 2 we performed a latge refactoring of the classes we had. Split the ones that used to perform too much tasks, organized the existing ones. At the end, our app used to work and look like something that actually can be used by people.

>

Iteration 3

Iteration 3

Optimization is the key

After the second iteration, we started to understand that despite the refactoring, our application still violates some SOLID principles, so it was a time for another big refactoring. We implemented the location as a service, separated the logic related to location processing from the UI classes. Now, we can be proud of what we did!

Settings

During this iteration we introduced the settings activity. No one had any experience to something like that, so it was a challenge. In the process, we realized that our structure needs a lot of modifications to be able to perform properly.

Final round

So, this is it. Here is the app. What else?
